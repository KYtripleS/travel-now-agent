## Voiceover Script *(platform-neutral, ~60 seconds)*

Most airport security delays aren't about the liquid rules themselves, they're caused by not having a system for them. The rules are simple: liquids in containers of 100ml or under, all in a single clear, resealable bag, and only one bag per passenger. The system that makes them easy is what most advice skips. First, use travel-size containers for everything. Full-size products above the limit are confiscated. Second, pack all your liquids in one clear bag; spreading them across multiple pouches creates delays. Third, place that clear bag at the top of your carry-on so you can remove it quickly. Finally, know what counts as a liquid at your airport – items like lip gloss, mascara, and even some deodorants are often classified as liquids. Solid toiletries, such as shampoo bars or solid sunscreen, typically don't count as liquids. A quick five-minute pre-flight check ensures all your containers are under the limit, fit in your bag, and that your bag is at the very top of your carry-on. The goal is to reach security, remove one bag in under 10 seconds, and move through without holding up the queue. This is achievable with just five minutes of prep the night before you fly.
