Why an eSIM for Your Travels?

For me, travel is about observation, presence, and adapting to new rhythms. While I appreciate being unplugged for stretches, there are practical moments when connectivity genuinely eases the way: looking up train schedules, navigating unfamiliar streets, or perhaps sharing a quiet moment with a loved one back home. An eSIM simplifies this. Instead of needing to find a local provider, swap out a physical SIM card, or worry about losing my home SIM, an eSIM allows me to activate a data plan digitally. It’s built right into my phone.

This convenience means I can land in a new country and often have my data working before I even clear customs, which is a small but meaningful way to reduce the initial overwhelm of arrival. It also means I can keep my home number active for calls or texts while using the eSIM for data, a balance I appreciate. Beyond the ease, eSIMs often offer clearer pricing than roaming, and they quietly align with my preference for mindful travel by reducing the need for single-use plastic. It helps me feel prepared, without needing to overthink.

1. Airalo — Best Overall Coverage and Simplicity

Airalo is a name many travelers find early in their eSIM search. Its main strength lies in its extensive global reach. If you’re planning a multi-country trip, or traveling to a less commonly covered destination, Airalo often has a plan for you. They offer local, regional, and global eSIMs, giving you flexibility depending on your itinerary.

Who it genuinely suits: This is often my recommendation for first-time eSIM users, or for anyone who values convenience and broad coverage most. If you’re hopping between a few countries in a region, or just want a single point of contact for all your data needs, Airalo streamlines the process. Their app is straightforward, making it simple to purchase, install, and manage your data plans. Topping up data is also quite easy within the app, which provides a reassuring safety net if your usage is higher than expected.

Where it is less strong: While Airalo’s coverage is extensive, it doesn’t always offer the absolute lowest per-GB price point, especially for shorter duration plans in popular countries where competition is high. If your priority is maximizing data at the lowest possible cost for a single, well-covered destination, it’s worth comparing.

What to check before buying: Always verify the specific coverage for your destination. Pay attention to the data allowance and the validity period. For instance, a 1GB plan might last 7 days, but if you’re there for two weeks and use your phone often, you’ll need to plan for a top-up or a larger initial package. Saily, a newer service from the creators of NordVPN, has found its niche by focusing on strong per-GB value. For travelers who keep a close eye on their budget and have a fairly good idea of their data needs, Saily can present a very attractive option. Who it genuinely suits: Saily can be a good choice if you prioritize value, especially for longer stays in a single country or a region where their coverage is strong. If you’re comfortable doing a quick price comparison and your primary goal is to get the most data for your dollar, Saily warrants a closer look. It’s also well-suited for travelers who might use their phone for moderate browsing, maps, and messaging, where knowing your data allowance helps manage costs.

Where it is less strong: As a newer service, Saily’s global coverage might not be as exhaustive as Airalo’s in every single niche destination. While their network is growing, for truly obscure locations, it’s always wise to double-check their specific country list. Their app, while functional, might not feel as feature-rich as some more established players for managing complex multi-eSIM scenarios.

What to check before buying: Focus on confirming coverage for your exact destination and comparing the per-GB cost for your required data amount and validity period. If you’re planning a trip of several weeks, Saily’s larger packages might offer excellent value. 3. Holafly — Best for Unlimited Data

Holafly is notable for its focus on unlimited data plans. For certain types of travelers, that removes a real chore: no watching a data meter, no fear of running dry mid-trip. (We put Airalo, Holafly and Saily side by side in our three-way comparison.)

Who it genuinely suits: This is an excellent option for heavy data users: digital nomads, remote workers, or anyone who plans to stream videos, participate in frequent video calls, or rely heavily on cloud services while traveling. If the thought of monitoring your data consumption feels like an unwelcome chore on your trip, Holafly’s unlimited plans can offer significant freedom. It’s particularly useful for those who want to ensure they always have internet access without fear of throttling due to reaching a cap.

Where it is less strong: Holafly’s primary consideration is the cost; unlimited plans often cost more than capped data from other providers. Also, while advertised as “unlimited,” it’s crucial to understand that fair use policies typically apply. This means that while you won’t be cut off, speeds might be reduced after a very high threshold of usage in a short period, though for most general travel purposes, this isn’t an issue. They are also primarily data-only plans, without a local phone number.

What to check before buying: Always read the fine print on their “unlimited” policies for your specific destination to understand any potential speed limitations or fair usage thresholds. Compare the cost against what you’d pay for a generous capped plan from another provider if you suspect your usage might not be truly extreme. Ensure they cover your specific destination with an unlimited plan.

Frequently asked questions

What is an eSIM and why should I use one?

An eSIM is a digital SIM card embedded in your phone, allowing you to activate a cellular plan without a physical SIM. It offers convenience for travelers by eliminating physical card swaps, enabling multiple plans on one device, and often providing more flexible and affordable data options abroad.

Which eSIM is best for general international travel?

For wide destination coverage and ease of use, Airalo is often a reliable choice. However, Saily can offer compelling per-GB value, especially for longer trips, and Holafly is good if you need truly unlimited data in specific countries.

Can I use an eSIM with my current phone?

Most newer smartphones (iPhone XS and later, many Samsung Galaxy S20 series and later, Google Pixel 3 and later) are eSIM compatible. Always check your specific phone model's specifications before purchasing an eSIM plan.

What should I check before buying an eSIM plan?

Confirm your device is eSIM compatible, check the specific country or region coverage, review the data allowance and expiry period, and understand the provider's fair use policy, especially for 'unlimited' plans.

Do eSIMs work for making calls and sending texts?

Most travel eSIMs are data-only. This means they are for internet access. You can still make calls and send texts using internet-based apps like WhatsApp, FaceTime, or Telegram. Some providers may offer plans with local numbers or calling minutes; always check the plan details.
