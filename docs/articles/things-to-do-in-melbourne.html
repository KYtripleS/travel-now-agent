Melbourne: The Places Worth Your Time

By Casey, Gently Yonder editor

A considered guide to Melbourne's places worth visiting — the CBD laneways and street art, Federation Square and the Yarra, the free NGV and Royal Botanic Gardens, Queen Victoria Market, St Kilda and its penguins, the MCG, and the Great Ocean Road.

Updated 2026-07-11 · 3 min read

Melbourne hides its pleasures rather than parading them — down laneways, inside galleries, along the bay — so the trick to a first visit is to slow down and let the city reveal itself. What follows is how I’d actually spend a few days, with the specific corners worth seeking out. Much of the best of it is free to wander; the ticketed sights and the big day trips are simplest to book on Klook or the Asia-strong KKday, and self-guided gallery and attraction tickets on Tiqets cover several highlights.

The laneways and street art

Start in the CBD laneways, the heart of Melbourne’s character — a maze of alleys threaded with cafés, tiny bars, and some of the world’s best street art. Hosier Lane, just off Federation Square, is the famous one, its bluestone walls a constantly repainted gallery; AC/DC Lane and Union Lane are close by. For a different mood, duck through the ornate Block Arcade and Royal Arcade — 19th-century glass-roofed shopping passages — and pause at Degraves Street for the classic laneway-café squeeze. This is a walk, not a ticket: wander, look up, and follow the coffee.

Federation Square and the State Library

Federation Square, the modern civic heart, sits where the grid meets the Yarra River and gathers the arts under one roof, including the Ian Potter Centre (Australian art). A few blocks north, don’t miss the State Library Victoria and its magnificent domed La Trobe Reading Room — free to enter, quietly breathtaking, and one of the loveliest interiors in the country.

The NGV and the gardens

The National Gallery of Victoria (NGV International) on St Kilda Road is the country’s oldest and most-visited gallery, and free for the permanent collection — lie on the floor beneath the stained-glass Great Hall ceiling, it’s the done thing. Nearby, the Royal Botanic Gardens roll down toward the river, beautiful for an hour or a picnic, with the Shrine of Remembrance on the rise above.

Queen Victoria Market

The sprawling Queen Victoria Market is a Melbourne institution — produce halls, the deli hall’s cheese and smallgoods, street food, and, in summer, a lively Wednesday night market of food stalls and bars. Go hungry, and early if you can; it’s a great place to feel the city’s multicultural pulse.

St Kilda and the bay

Out on the bay, St Kilda brings faded-seaside charm: the beach and St Kilda Pier, the old Luna Park with its grinning-face entrance, the cake shops of Acland Street, and — a lovely free surprise — a colony of little penguins that waddles ashore on the pier’s breakwater at dusk (go quietly, no flash). A little further round the bay, the candy-coloured Brighton Bathing Boxes are the postcard shot.

Sport, and the MCG

Melbourne is a sport city, and the Melbourne Cricket Ground (MCG) is close to sacred; a stadium-and-museum tour, or a match if the season suits — cricket in summer, AFL in winter — is a real window into the culture. In January the Australian Open brings the tennis world to Melbourne Park next door.

A big day out

When you have a full day, the Great Ocean Road — the dramatic coastal drive to the Twelve Apostles sea stacks — is the region’s headline outing, long but rewarding and easily done as a guided day tour. The alternatives: the Yarra Valley for wineries and the Healesville wildlife sanctuary, or Phillip Island for its nightly penguin parade.

How to hold a first visit

Give each day a loose anchor — the laneways and the NGV, the market and the gardens, St Kilda and the bay, one big day trip — and leave room to sit in a café and watch the city go by, which is, after all, the local pastime. For getting between it, see our Getting Around Melbourne guide and the first-timer’s guide to Melbourne. Opening hours and prices shift, so confirm the specifics close to your visit.

Frequently asked questions

What are the Melbourne laneways and are they worth it?

The CBD laneways are a maze of alleys full of cafés, tiny bars, and some of the world's best street art — Hosier Lane is the famous one. They are the heart of Melbourne's character and free to wander; just look up and follow the coffee.

Is the National Gallery of Victoria free?

The NGV's permanent collection is free to enter. It is the country's oldest and most-visited gallery, split across two sites, and a genuine highlight — with the Royal Botanic Gardens nearby for an hour's reset.

Can you see penguins in Melbourne?

Yes — a colony of little penguins comes ashore on the St Kilda breakwater at dusk, a lovely free surprise. St Kilda also has the beach and pier and the old Luna Park, making an easy half-day out on the bay.

Is the Great Ocean Road a good day trip from Melbourne?

Yes — the Great Ocean Road, the dramatic coastal drive to the Twelve Apostles, is the region's headline outing. It is a long day but rewarding, and easily done as a guided day tour if you'd rather not hire a car and drive it yourself.
