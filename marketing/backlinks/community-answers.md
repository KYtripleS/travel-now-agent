## Q: "Best eSIM for Japan?" (r/JapanTravel, r/JapanTravelTips, Quora)

Japan has excellent coverage almost everywhere, so you don't need anything fancy —
the main choice is data amount, not network. For a typical 7–10 day trip with maps,
messaging, and some browsing, 3–5GB is plenty; heavy video/hotspot users want 10GB+
or an unlimited plan. Two marketplaces cover Japan well: **Airalo** (widest country
list, easy top-ups) and **Saily** (often a bit cheaper per GB). Install it *before*
you fly but leave it switched off until you land — that way you're not depending on
airport Wi-Fi to set it up. One gotcha: make sure your phone is carrier-unlocked and
eSIM-capable first.

## Q: "Pocket WiFi vs eSIM for Japan/Asia?" (r/JapanTravel, r/digitalnomad)

Rough rule: **solo or light traveler → eSIM; family/group or heavy multi-device →
pocket WiFi.** An eSIM has nothing to carry, nothing to return, and no battery to
babysit, but it only connects your one phone. Pocket WiFi shares one connection
across several devices (great for a family), but it's another gadget to charge,
carry, and hand back — and if it dies, *everyone's* offline. For one or two people
who mostly need maps and messaging, an eSIM is simpler and usually cheaper. Two
travelers with laptops who want to tether all day often come out ahead with pocket
WiFi.

## Q: "SafetyWing vs World Nomads — which for long-term travel?" (r/digitalnomad, r/solotravel, Quora)

They suit different travelers. **SafetyWing** is a rolling 28-day subscription — you
start it, it renews until you cancel, no fixed return date needed. That flexibility
plus the lower price is why nomads like it; the trade-off is thinner trip-cancellation
and baggage cover. **World Nomads** is fixed-term per trip and stronger on adventure
activities and non-medical benefits (cancellation, gear), but you buy a new policy for
each trip. If you're medically-focused and open-ended, SafetyWing; if you've got
pre-paid bookings and adventurous plans on a defined trip, World Nomads. Whichever you
pick, read the exclusions on pre-existing conditions and your specific activities —
that's where claims actually get denied.

## Q: "What should I pack for 10 days in Japan? Carry-on only doable?" (r/JapanTravel, r/onebag)

Very doable carry-on-only, even in shoulder season. The trick is a 10-item capsule in
one colour family + doing laundry once (coin laundries are everywhere). Japan-specific
notes that catch people out: it still runs on **cash** more than most big countries, so
carry some; get a **Suica/PASMO IC card** (or Apple Pay transit) day one; you'll walk
~15,000 steps a day, so bring *already broken-in* shoes, not new ones; and pack a modest
layer if you're visiting temples. Type A plugs (same as the US) at 100V, so US chargers
just work; everyone else needs a plug adapter.

## Q: "Where can I store luggage in Tokyo for a day?" (r/JapanTravel)

A few options depending on where you are. **Coin lockers** in major stations (Tokyo,
Shinjuku, Shibuya, Ueno, Asakusa) are cheapest, including big "baggage" sizes — but they
fill up fast near tourist spots on weekends, so go early. If lockers are full, **luggage-
storage apps/networks** let nearby shops and cafés hold bags by the day, and most
**hotels will hold your bags free** before check-in or after check-out. For moving bags
between hotels or to the airport, **takkyubin** (door-to-door delivery) is cheap and
reliable — a lot of travelers send the big bag ahead and explore hands-free.
