# Digital PR: expert-quote pitches (Featured / Qwoted / SourceBottle / HARO-style)

This is the highest-ROI, cleanest backlink source: reporters request expert quotes,
you send a good one, they publish it with a link to gentlyyonder.com. Editorial
links from real publications — exactly what Google rewards.

## How to run it (15 min/day)
1. Make free accounts on **Featured.com**, **Qwoted**, **SourceBottle** (and any
   current HARO successor). Set your topic alerts to: travel, packing, digital
   nomad, travel insurance, eSIM, airports, Japan.
2. When a relevant query lands, reply fast (early = higher chance) with ONE tight,
   specific, genuinely useful answer. No fluff, no sales pitch.
3. Sign every response with the byline block below.
4. Log sends in `outreach-log.md`.

## Byline block (paste at the bottom of every pitch)
> **Casey — Editor, Gently Yonder** (https://gentlyyonder.com)
> Gently Yonder is an independent travel-preparation publication covering eSIMs,
> packing, airport security, and travel insurance.
> Contact: @TripWorldAdvice on X.

(Pen name + brand only — never a real name. This is standard for publications.)

## Pre-written responses (personalize the opening line each time)

### Topic: eSIM / staying connected abroad
"The mistake travelers make is buying the biggest data plan out of fear. For a
typical week abroad with maps, messaging, and light browsing, 3–5GB is plenty;
only heavy hotspot users need unlimited. Install the eSIM before you fly but leave
it switched off until you land — that avoids depending on airport Wi-Fi to activate
it. And check two things first: your phone must be carrier-unlocked and eSIM-capable."

### Topic: travel insurance / what people get wrong
"Most travelers compare the headline price and skip the exclusions — which is exactly
where claims get denied. Before buying, read three things: whether pre-existing
conditions are covered, whether your specific activities (scuba, motorbikes, skiing)
are included or need an add-on, and the per-item baggage limit. A subscription-style
policy suits open-ended trips; a fixed-term policy is usually stronger for pre-paid
bookings and adventure travel."

### Topic: airport security / getting through faster
"Pack in the order the X-ray expects and you'll rarely get pulled aside. Liquids in a
single clear bag near the top; laptop and large tablet easy to grab; and remember
power banks and spare lithium batteries must be in your carry-on, never checked —
that's the single most common reason a checked bag gets held. Empty every pocket
before the scanner, not at it."

### Topic: packing light / carry-on only
"Carry-on-only for two weeks is a colour-palette problem, not a volume problem. Pick
one colour family so everything mixes, cap it around ten pieces, and plan to do
laundry once rather than packing for every day. The two things worth their weight:
broken-in shoes and a universal adapter with USB-C."

### Topic: Japan travel tips (our strongest cluster)
"Two things surprise first-time visitors to Japan: how much still runs on cash, and
how much you'll walk — easily 15,000 steps a day. Carry some cash, get a Suica or
PASMO IC card on arrival, and bring shoes you've already broken in. For temples like
Sensō-ji, a modest layer saves you second-guessing at the gate."

## Notes
- Keep each answer 40–90 words; editors trim, and tight quotes get used.
- Never send the same wording twice verbatim — reword the opening at minimum.
- One published quote on a real travel/finance site can outweigh months of SEO grind.
