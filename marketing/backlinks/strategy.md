# Gently Yonder — backlink & authority playbook (white-hat only)

Backlinks still move rankings more than almost anything else, but the *wrong*
way tanks you (Google penalties, subreddit bans, wasted months). This is the
honest version: earn links by being genuinely useful, and build linkable
assets people *want* to cite.

## The anonymity rule
All outreach uses the pen name **Casey, editor at Gently Yonder** + the brand.
Never the operator's real name. A brand + pen name is completely normal for
digital PR and is what most publications expect.

## ✅ What actually works (do these)

1. **Digital PR / journalist quotes (highest ROI).** Reporters constantly need
   expert quotes on travel-prep topics. Answer their queries with a short,
   genuinely useful quote and they link "Casey, editor at Gently Yonder
   (gentlyyonder.com)". These are *editorial* links from real publications —
   the gold standard. Platforms: **Featured.com** (formerly Terkel),
   **Qwoted**, **SourceBottle**, **HARO's successors**. See `haro-pitches.md`.

2. **Genuinely helpful community answers.** On Reddit/Quora, *answer the
   question properly first.* A link is a bonus, only where allowed, only when
   it genuinely adds more than the answer already did. See `community-answers.md`.
   This builds brand recognition even when a link isn't allowed.

3. **Linkable assets (build once, earn links for years).** We already have
   several — lean into them:
   - the free **printable Pre-Flight Checklist PDF**
   - the **Trip Checklist Generator** tool
   - the **power-adapter-by-country** reference pages
   - the honest **X vs Y comparison** articles
   Bloggers and forum users cite tools and reference data far more than opinion posts.

4. **Resource-page & roundup outreach.** Many travel sites have "best travel
   resources" pages. A short, personal email suggesting our checklist PDF or a
   comparison as a genuinely useful addition earns contextual links.

5. **Be where travelers already gather, consistently.** Steady, helpful presence
   in a few communities > one spammy blast.

## 🚫 What NOT to do (these hurt you)
- ❌ Dropping links in Reddit/Quora without answering — instant spam flag / ban.
- ❌ Buying links, PBNs, "1000 backlinks for $5" — Google penalty.
- ❌ Blog-comment / forum-signature spam.
- ❌ Fake accounts or sockpuppets.
- ❌ Exact-match anchor text stuffing ("best eSIM Japan" over and over).
Google's link spam systems (SpamBrain) catch these and can suppress the whole site.

## Realistic targets & pace
- New domain (weeks old): focus on **1-3 quality links/month**, not volume.
- 5 genuine editorial/community links > 500 spam links.
- Track what you send in `outreach-log.md` (create as you go): date, platform,
  topic, link status.

## The subreddit reality (read before posting)
- **r/travel, r/solotravel** — strict self-promo rules; links often removed.
  Participate genuinely; do NOT lead with your link. Value first.
- **r/JapanTravel, r/JapanTravelTips** — very active; great for our Japan
  cluster, but same rule: answer helpfully, link only if it clearly helps and
  the sub allows it.
- **r/digitalnomad, r/onebag** — good fit for eSIM/insurance/packing, but
  self-promo-sensitive.
Read each sub's rules + check if mods allow links before posting. When unsure,
help without the link — the brand mention still compounds.
