# Travel Now — Welcome email sequence (drafts)

Paste these into whatever email tool you connect (Tally → Mailerlite / ConvertKit /
Beehiiv / Buttondown all work). Three emails, sent over the first week. Keep the
sender name **Travel Now** and reply-to an address you actually read.

Tone rules (match the site): plain, useful, no hype, no "hey traveler!!". One idea
per email. Affiliate links only where genuinely useful, always with the same FTC
disclosure line at the foot.

Standard disclosure footer to append to every email:

> Travel Now sometimes uses affiliate links. If you book or buy through one, we may
> earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. It never changes what we recommend.
> You're getting this because you downloaded our Pre-Flight Checklist. Unsubscribe anytime.

## Email 1 — send immediately on signup

**Subject:** Your Pre-Flight Checklist (+ the one thing most people forget)
**Preview text:** Two pages, printable, and the rule that catches travelers out.

Hi,

Here's the checklist you asked for — two pages, printable, fridge-worthy:

**→ Download the Pre-Flight Checklist (PDF):**
https://kytriples.github.io/travel-now-agent/downloads/travel-now-preflight-checklist.pdf

Print it, or just keep it on your phone. Page 1 is the night-before run-through.
Page 2 is the security rules cheat-sheet — the liquid and battery rules that get
people pulled aside.

The one most travelers forget: **power banks and spare lithium batteries have to be
in your carry-on, never checked.** It's a global rule, and a checked bag with a loose
battery can get held back. Page 2 covers the rest.

That's it for now. In a couple of days I'll send you the short list of prep guides
worth reading before a trip — the ones that actually save money.

Safe travels,

## Notes for setup

- **Deliverability:** send from a real domain if you can; a free-mail sender hurts inbox
  placement. Even a cheap custom domain on the ESP's sending infra helps.
- **Cadence after the welcome series:** one email a week is plenty. Re-use the best
  sections of new articles as the body — don't write the newsletter from scratch.
- **The reply ask in Email 3 is the highest-value line in the whole sequence.** Replies
  train the inbox provider that you're wanted mail, and the answers tell you what to
  write next. Don't cut it.
