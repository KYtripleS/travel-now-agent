Who writes Gently Yonder

Meet Casey

The editor behind Gently Yonder's prep guides — the unglamorous half of travel, handled once, properly, so you can forget about it.

Casey

Editor, Gently Yonder · writes about the practical side of travel

Hi — I'm Casey. I edit Gently Yonder, which means I spend an unreasonable amount of time reading airline battery rules, eSIM fine print, and immigration FAQs so that you don't have to. My job is the boring, important half of a trip: the part that goes wrong at the gate when nobody prepared for it.

A note on the name

"Casey" is a pen name. Gently Yonder is run by a small team that prefers to stay out of the frame, so we publish under one editor's name — it keeps the voice consistent and the attention on the guides rather than on us. We'd rather be judged on whether the advice is accurate and useful than on a personality. So the "I" you read is a narrative voice — a device to keep these guides warm and readable — not a claim about one real person's documented trips. The opinions are genuinely held, and, whatever the voice around them, the facts never bend: prices, rules, and specifications are always verifiable.

What I cover

  • Connectivity — eSIMs, pocket WiFi, and landing with data that works.
  • The airport — security rules, liquids, batteries, carry-on order.
  • Insurance — what's actually covered, and the exclusions that bite.
  • Packing — capsule wardrobes, everyday carry, and what to leave home.
  • Places — layered country profiles and city guides for travelers who want context, not clichés.

How I work

I read the primary sources first — the aviation authority, the provider's own terms, the official statistics — and I tell you plainly where my confidence comes from. I write in the first person because it's the honest way to share a point of view, but I never bend a fact to fit a story: prices, rules, and specifications stay verifiable whatever the voice around them. The full version of this is on our Methodology page, and the standards every article follows are in our Editorial Guidelines.

The guide I want to write is the one I wish I'd had open the night before the flight — specific, honest, and finished in ten minutes.
