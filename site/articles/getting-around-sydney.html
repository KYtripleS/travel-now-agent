Getting Around Sydney: Opal Card, Trains, Ferries & Buses

By Casey, Gently Yonder editor

A practical guide to Sydney public transport — the Opal card and contactless fares, trains as the fast backbone, the harbour ferries, buses to the beaches, light rail, and the honest options from Sydney Airport.

Updated 2026-07-10 · 3 min read

Build My Trip Checklist All guides

Sydney is a harbour city, and getting around it is genuinely one of the pleasures of a visit — not least because some of the “public transport” is a ferry gliding past the Opera House. The network is easy once you know the basics, and one system ties it together. Two things worth sorting early smooth the whole trip: a travel eSIM so maps and timetables work the moment you land, and — if you’d rather have your first airport run handled — a pre-booked private airport pickup.

Opal, or just your bank card

Sydney runs on Opal, the contactless tap-on, tap-off system that covers trains, the Metro, buses, ferries, and light rail across greater Sydney. The nice part: you don’t need a physical Opal card at all — you can tap a contactless bank card or phone directly, for the same fare. Either way the fares are capped: a daily cap and a weekly cap limit what you’ll ever pay, and a much cheaper Sunday cap makes a Sunday of ferry-hopping absurdly good value. Tap on when you board and off when you leave (miss the tap-off and you’re charged the maximum), and the system does the maths. Fares and caps change, so check current Opal pricing before you go.

Trains and the Metro: the fast backbone

The Sydney Trains network — clean double-deckers — is the quickest way to cover distance, linking the CBD, the inner suburbs, and out to the greater region. The newer, driverless Sydney Metro adds a fast, frequent line through the centre. It’s the sensible way in from the airport too: the Airport Link train runs from both the domestic and international terminals into the city in about 15 minutes — but note it adds a station access fee on top of the normal fare, so with a group or heavy bags a taxi or a booked pickup can work out comparable.

Ferries: the best-value sightseeing in the city

If you do one thing, ride a ferry from Circular Quay. They’re ordinary commuter boats at ordinary Opal fares, but the routes are spectacular — the F1 to Manly in particular is a half-hour harbour cruise past the Opera House and out to the Heads for the price of a bus ticket. (There’s also a faster private Manly Fast Ferry if you’re in a hurry, at a higher fare.) Ferries also reach Taronga Zoo, Watsons Bay, Cockatoo Island, and the inner-harbour suburbs. It is, quietly, the best-value sightseeing in Sydney — skip the paid “harbour cruises” and just ride the green-and-gold boats.

Buses and light rail

Buses fill in everywhere the trains and ferries don’t, including the run out to Bondi Beach (take a train to Bondi Junction, then the 333 bus). The light rail connects Central Station through the CBD to Circular Quay on the L1, and out to the eastern suburbs (Randwick and Kingsford) on the L2 and L3 — handy for short city hops and reaching the SCG or Randwick. Neither needs much planning: tap on, check the next stop, tap off.

From the airport, honestly

Your options from Sydney Airport are the Airport Link train (fastest to the CBD, but with that access fee), regular buses, taxis and rideshare from the ranks, or a booked private pickup for a name-board and a fixed price after a long flight. For a solo traveller heading to the CBD, the train is usually the easy pick; for a family with luggage, a car often wins on both hassle and cost.

A simple mental model

Hold this and you’re set: Opal (or your bank card) for everything, trains and Metro for distance, ferries for the harbour, buses for the beaches. Get connected with an eSIM, tap your way around, and treat at least one ferry as sightseeing rather than transport. Frequently asked questions

Do I need an Opal card in Sydney?

Not necessarily — you can tap a contactless bank card or phone directly on trains, buses, ferries, and light rail for the same fare as a physical Opal card. Either way there are daily and weekly caps, plus a cheaper Sunday cap that makes weekend exploring good value.

What is the best-value way to see Sydney Harbour?

Ride a ferry from Circular Quay. They are ordinary commuter boats at normal Opal fares, but the routes are spectacular — the Manly ferry is a half-hour harbour cruise past the Opera House and the Heads for the price of a bus ticket.

How do I get from Sydney Airport to the city?

The Airport Link train reaches the CBD in around 15 minutes but carries a station access fee on top of the fare; buses, taxis and rideshare, or a pre-booked private pickup are alternatives. Solo to the CBD, the train usually wins; with a family and luggage, a car is often comparable on cost and easier.

How do I get to Bondi Beach on public transport?

Bondi is reached by bus, simple with Opal or a contactless card. A common route is a train to Bondi Junction and then a short bus to the beach.
